# Commercial Licensing

NISB is available under the GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPLv3) for open-source use.

Commercial licensing is available for individuals, teams, companies, institutions, and organizations that need terms different from AGPLv3.

This document is provided for project transparency and does not constitute legal advice.

---

## When you may need a commercial license

You may need a commercial license if you want to:

- Build a proprietary product based on NISB.
- Build a closed-source integration based on NISB.
- Embed NISB into a commercial product under non-AGPL terms.
- Modify NISB and provide it as a hosted or SaaS service without using AGPLv3 terms.
- Distribute a modified version under non-AGPL terms.
- Privately redistribute modified versions under custom terms.
- Integrate NISB into proprietary infrastructure under terms different from AGPLv3.
- Build commercial products, private deployments, or enterprise workflows based on NISB under custom terms.
- Negotiate custom enterprise, partnership, investment, integration, deployment, or redistribution terms.

A commercial license is not required merely because you use NISB at work or in a business setting, as long as your use complies with AGPLv3.

If you are unsure whether your use case complies with AGPLv3, consult legal counsel.

---

## What commercial licensing can include

Depending on the written agreement, a commercial license may include:

- Alternative licensing terms.
- Proprietary integration rights.
- Private redistribution rights.
- Hosted or SaaS rights.
- Enterprise deployment terms.
- Team or organization terms.
- Custom integration terms.
- Custom redistribution terms.
- Partnership terms.
- Investment or strategic collaboration terms.

Commercial license scope is defined only by the final written agreement.

---

## Paid services are separate

Paid services may be offered separately from software licensing.

Paid services may include:

- Remote Install.
- Migration support.
- Private deployment help.
- Deployment consulting.
- Priority support.
- Training or onboarding.
- Custom workflow design.
- MCP, RAG, Room, or document-processing integration consulting.

Paying for services does not automatically grant a commercial software license.

A commercial software license requires a separate written agreement.

Remote Install orders are installation services only. They do not include custom feature development, long-term managed hosting, production SLA, private redistribution rights, hosted/SaaS rights, closed-source commercial licensing, or third-party dependency commercial licenses.

## What a commercial license does not include by default

Unless explicitly stated in a separate written agreement, a NISB commercial license does not automatically include:

- Ownership of NISB.
- Transfer of copyright.
- Equity.
- Partnership.
- Employment relationship.
- Agency relationship.
- Revenue share.
- Governance rights.
- Exclusive rights.
- Resale rights.
- Trademark rights.
- Logo or branding rights.
- Patent rights from third parties.
- Commercial rights for third-party dependencies.
- API provider subscriptions.
- Model provider subscriptions.
- Cloud infrastructure fees.
- Managed hosting.
- Production SLA.
- Custom feature development.
- Legal review.

These require separate written agreements, separate service agreements, or separate upstream licenses.

## Third-party dependencies

A NISB commercial license covers only NISB rights that the maintainer owns or is legally able to license.

It does not automatically grant commercial, proprietary, hosted, SaaS, embedded, redistribution, patent, trademark, or other special rights for third-party dependencies.

Third-party dependencies remain governed by their own licenses.

Some dependencies may have their own commercial licensing requirements.

In particular, PyMuPDF / MuPDF and pymupdf4llm may require special attention because they are commonly distributed under AGPL/commercial dual licensing.

If a commercial, proprietary, hosted, SaaS, embedded, closed-source, or redistributed deployment uses PyMuPDF / MuPDF / pymupdf4llm functionality, the customer, distributor, or operator is responsible for ensuring compliance with the applicable upstream license terms.

See:

- `COMMERCIAL-THIRD-PARTY-DEPENDENCIES.md`
- `THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md`

## Trademark and branding

The open-source license does not grant trademark, logo, branding, domain name, or endorsement rights except as required to identify the original project.

Use of the NISB name, logo, branding, domain name, or project identity in a commercial product, hosted service, marketplace listing, modified distribution, or public integration may require separate written permission.

---

## No automatic partnership

A commercial license does not create a partnership, employment relationship, agency relationship, investment relationship, revenue share, governance right, or ownership interest.

Any such arrangement requires a separate written agreement.
